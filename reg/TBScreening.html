Tuberculosis Screening

If you plan to volunteer in the school in ANY capacity, please read this!

The Contra Costa Health Department REQUIRES all volunteers in schools to show that they are free of communicable tuberculosis (TB). This applies to adults who volunteer in the school in any capacity, whether in the classroom, on field trips, or in the hot lunch program.

TB screening consists of a PPD skin test every four years. All PPD skin tests need to be checked by a trained health professional 2 to 3 days after they are given. THE TEST IS VALID ONLY AFTER BOTH STEPS ARE COMPLETE. The Orinda Union School District maintains a list of parents with up-to-date TB tests, which is available in the school office.

What You Need To Do

  • Provide proof of negative PPD within the last 4 years. This must be kept updated and on file at Glorietta.
  • A volunteer with a positive PPD needs to provide proof of a negative chest X-ray and negative symptom screening by health care provider stating volunteer is free of communicable TB.
  • You may obtain TB screening from your private health care provider, the Contra Costa Clinics or from any of the schools listed below.
