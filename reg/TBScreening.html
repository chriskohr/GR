Tuberculosis Screening

If you plan to volunteer in the school in ANY capacity, please read this!

The Contra Costa Health Department REQUIRES all volunteers in schools to show that they are free of communicable tuberculosis (TB). This applies to adults who volunteer in the school in any capacity, whether in the classroom, on field trips, or in the hot lunch program.

TB screening consists of a PPD skin test every four years. All PPD skin tests need to be checked by a trained health professional 2 to 3 days after they are given.

Click Here to see the List of Parents current with their TB Testing.

THE COST FOR THE TB TEST IS $20. PAYMENT IS EXPECTED ON THE DATE OF THE TEST. Cash or checks are accepted.

What You Need To Do

  • All volunteers must update this information every four years. This information must be kept on file at the school.
  • Volunteers need to provide the school with proof of a recent negative PPD skin test.
  • A volunteer with a positive PPD needs to provide the school with proof of a negative chest X-ray and a recent symptom screening by a physician, stating that the volunteer is free of communicable TB.
  • You can obtain a PPD or proof of recent chest X-ray from your private physician.
  • You can also receive a PPD skin test at the Contra Costa Immunization Clinics.
